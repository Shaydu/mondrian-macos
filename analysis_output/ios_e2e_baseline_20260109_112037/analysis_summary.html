Ansel Adams (1902-1984)

Adams was a master of large-format landscape photography and a pioneer of photographic modernism in America. His technical innovations, particularly the Zone System for exposure and development, gave photographers unprecedented control over tonality and detail. His iconic images of Yosemite and the American West established landscape photography as a fine art form and inspired generations of photographers to see nature as a subject for both technical mastery and spiritual expression.
