Ansel Adams

1902-1984

Ansel Adams (1902-1984) was an American landscape photographer and environmentalist known for his black-and-white images of the American West, especially Yosemite National Park. He helped found the photography group f/64, developed the Zone System for achieving perfect exposure and tonal range, and was a pioneering advocate for environmental conservation through his art.

Review Focus

  • Tonal Range & Zone System: Master of the full spectrum from pure black to pure white, using his Zone System to achieve perfect exposure and tonal gradation in every image.
  • Composition & Perspective: Precise framing that emphasizes geometric elements, leading lines, and careful positioning to create powerful visual impact.
  • Technical Excellence: Perfect sharpness, focus, and exposure achieved through large-format cameras and meticulous darkroom work.
  • Landscape Drama: Captures the majesty and drama of natural landscapes with emphasis on light, shadow, and texture.
