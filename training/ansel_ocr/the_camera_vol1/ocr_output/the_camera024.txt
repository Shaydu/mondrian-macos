Small-Format Cameras

The modern small camera can function as an extension of the eye
in “reaching out” into the world. The flow of life, the rapidly
changing relationships of objects and realities, seem to come into an
embrace with the photographer's eye and imagination. This view
of the world is far more fluent than is possible with a
Yet it is this very fluency that is the greatest challenge of small:
format photography, for the photographer is called upon to assess
the moving elements of a scene and integrate them into an effective
still photograph in fractions of a second.
